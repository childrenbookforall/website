I am an *autistic* person, also with *anxiety*. I am a very *determined* person. I deal with *discrimination* and *ableism*, but I also don’t give up. Music, dancing, movies, TV shows, nature, food etc. gives me happiness. Any ableism or discrimination makes me *sad*. Being misunderstood for who I am makes me sad. I am generally a *sensitive* person. I feel very much even though I don’t really show it.  

My relationship with *children’s books* connect me with my *inner child*. It *heals* me and makes me feel good. I started reading children’s book when I met Arushi. She used to share Children’s books with me. I was *surprised* to see how much it is *relevant* to me - how much I can still learn about life and how therapeutic it is. Each of our readings have been very *special*. It brings me closer to the community.  

![Swara with Ruby's worry](https://ik.imagekit.io/ty6gikkxo/swara-ruby.jpeg?updatedAt=1741352180477)
<span class="image-title">Working through my worries with Ruby's worry</span>

I am happy that reading Children’s books are a part of my life. I hope to continue to read them whenever I feel down, whenever I need *motivation*. I just want to say, you all are missing a lot! Reading children’s books are so *therapeutic*. You can learn so much from it. We often think that children’s books are childish, but there is always a *message*. So please don’t let go off children’s books.
