<span class="image-title">The book: Why Johnny Doesn't Flap</span>

As part of celebrating World Autism Awareness Month, CBA read Why Johnny Doesn’t Flap — a powerful book that reverses the usual narrative around neurodiversity. Instead of portraying neurodivergent (ND) individuals as the ones who are “different,” the story frames neurotypical (NT) behavior as the outlier, inviting readers to reflect on perspective and bias.

Through this reversal, the book gently but firmly challenges the idea that ND individuals are solely responsible for adapting or being understood. It brings to light the importance of inclusion, respect, and shared responsibility in relationships. By centering the ND experience without pathologizing it, the book encourages us to see ND folx as whole, valuable, and worthy of being seen as they are — not only in spite of their differences, but because of them.

The reading was facilitated by Devrupa Rakshit and Swara Swami, both individuals on the autism spectrum, who brought lived experience and thoughtful insight into the space. They began by inviting everyone to share the first word that came to mind when they heard neurodiversity. The responses reflected a wide range of reflections — from accepting differences and respecting choices to a powerful theme of being outside the box. At the same time, we closed the reading with what shifts they observe in themselves after reading the children’s book. This circle set a tone of openness and grounding the reading in personal connection and community wisdom.
