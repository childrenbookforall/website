### *Dor* | डोर : A reflective support circle for anyone experiencing loneliness and seeking to build meaningful connections

## About Group
Over **8 sessions across 4 months**, each meeting will explore themes that speak to belonging and identity, queer connections, or unspoken experiences of loneliness, grief, change, isolation, boredom, and rebuilding a relationship with oneself and the world.  

The journey will be supported by poetry, art, specially curated children’s books, and the company of each other, with conversations and reflective activities that invite play, meaning-making, connection, and shared wisdom.  

The circle will be facilitated by therapists **Anoushka Malviya** and **Sayali Sontakke**, who are part of the CBA community.

## The Facilitators

**Anoushka Malviya**  

Anoushka Malviya (she/they) is a psychotherapist whose work is informed by relational-psychodynamic and somatic approaches. She believes mental health is shaped by socio-cultural and political contexts. Her interest is in the emotional life of relationships; intimacy, longing, distance, disconnection, dependence, and the struggle many people experience in feeling emotionally met. Many people carry loneliness privately, often with shame, as though it reflects something personal about their worth or ability to connect. But loneliness is deeply relational; it has to do with longing, connection, attunement, belonging, and sometimes grief.
Through Dor, she hopes to make room for reflective and supportive space where people can think about loneliness together, speak about it honestly, and explore their relationship to connection, belonging, and emotional presence.  

**Sayali Sontakke**  

Saai/Sayali (they/them) is a Mental Health Worker and expressive arts therapy practitioner who incorporates person-centered, narrative, trauma-focused, and intersectional feminist approaches in their work. Their journey with loneliness started at age five when moving to a new city introduced them to the challenging experience of starting over. Loneliness later became a transient companion, guiding them toward a deeper understanding of themselves and helping them prioritize their needs. For Saai, loneliness is not just about feeling isolated; it evokes a deep sense of belonging, a fundamental need that connects us with ourselves, our loved ones, our animal companions, and the multitude of living beings around us. With Dor, individuals can come together to listen, share, and reflect. It's a place where stories can be shared and reimagined, and where a collective practice of compassion and care flourishes.
